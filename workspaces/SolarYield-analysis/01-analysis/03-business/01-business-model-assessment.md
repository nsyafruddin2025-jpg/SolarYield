# Business Model Assessment (20%)

## Score: 2 / 10

## Evidence

### What exists

- Pipeline produces yield data at a known cost (Open-Meteo free tier, PVLib open-source)
- Singapore target market identified
- General context: C&I solar growing in Southeast Asia

### What is missing

- **No revenue model**: Is this SaaS subscription? Per-site license? Per-MWh reported? Outcome-based?
- **No pricing**: What does a customer pay, how much, how often?
- **No cost structure**: What does it cost to serve 1 customer? 100 customers? 1,000?
- **No go-to-market**: Direct sales? Channel partners? Online self-service?
- **No customer segmentation**: Commercial rooftops? Utility-scale? Industrial parks? Government?
- **No sales cycle**: 1-week close or 18-month enterprise procurement?
- **No switching cost**: Why would a customer pay for this vs. building it in-house or using a free spreadsheet?
- **No competitive moat**: What stops a competitor from copying this exact approach?
- **No partnership analysis**: Could this be sold through solar panel installers ( EPCs), inverter vendors, or grid operators?
- **No regulatory monetization**: Could yield reports be sold to banks financing solar projects (bankable data)?

## Single Biggest Gap

**No answer to "how does this make money."** Every business model decision flows from the revenue model: pricing drives customer segmentation, segmentation drives GTM, GTM drives the product features that matter. Building a technically correct pipeline with no clarity on who pays, for what, and why is the classic "build it and they won't come" failure mode.

## Business Model Hypotheses to Validate

The most viable path appears to be **bankable yield reporting for C&I solar financing**:

- Banks require investor-grade yield reports to finance solar projects
- Existing solutions are expensive ($50K+ setup, proprietary)
- A validated pipeline with physical accuracy documentation could undercut incumbents
- Revenue: per-report fee or annual subscription
- This aligns with the technical strength (accurate PVLib-based modeling) and the Singapore market (highly regulated, bank-dependent financing)

This needs immediate customer discovery to validate.
